Vinegar Coleslaw Recipe

Description

This Amish-style slaw is a crisp, tangy cabbage salad dressed in a sweet and zesty vinegar-and-oil based dressing made from white
vinegar, vegetable oil, sugar, celery seed, mustard, salt, and pepper, mixed with shredded cabbage and sliced onion; it takes about
15 minutes to prepare and cook the dressing and around 20 minutes total, and while it can be enjoyed right away, letting it chill
a couple of hours enhances the flavor and texture into a refreshing side dish perfect for picnics, barbecues, or meals that need a
bright, crunchy accompaniment.

Ingredients

  • 1 large head cabbage, cored and finely shredded
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • ¾ cup vinegar
  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons celery seed
  • 1 ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon prepared mustard
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Toss cabbage and onion together in a large bowl; set aside.
  3. Combine sugar, vinegar, oil, celery seed, salt, mustard,
    and pepper in a small saucepan; bring to a boil and
    simmer, stirring frequently, until sugar
    is dissolved, about 3 minutes.
  4. Let mixture cool slightly, about 5 minutes,
    then pour over cabbage mixture; toss well to coat.
  5. Serve and enjoy!
